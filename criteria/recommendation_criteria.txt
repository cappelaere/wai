# Recommendation Profile Evaluation Criteria

This file contains additional criteria that should be considered when evaluating recommendation letters for this scholarship.

## Evaluation Focus Areas

1. **Recommender Credibility and Relationship**
   - Prioritize recommendations from flight school instructors, especially CFIs (Certified Flight Instructors)
   - Value recommendations from aviation professionals (pilots, ATC, maintenance, etc.)
   - Consider the duration and depth of the recommender's relationship with the applicant
   - Assess the recommender's ability to evaluate the applicant's aviation skills and potential

2. **Aviation-Specific Endorsements**
   - Look for specific examples of the applicant's flying skills, safety awareness, and aviation knowledge
   - Value recommendations that demonstrate the applicant's commitment to aviation excellence
   - Consider endorsements related to the applicant's potential for success in aviation careers
   - Assess mentions of the applicant's ability to handle challenges in aviation training

3. **Character and Professional Qualities**
   - Look for evidence of reliability, responsibility, and professionalism
   - Value mentions of leadership, teamwork, and communication skills
   - Consider endorsements of the applicant's work ethic and dedication
   - Assess recommendations that highlight the applicant's ability to learn and adapt

4. **Consistency and Depth**
   - Value consistency in themes across multiple recommendations
   - Look for specific examples and anecdotes rather than generic praise
   - Consider the depth of knowledge the recommender demonstrates about the applicant
   - Assess whether recommendations provide concrete evidence of the applicant's strengths

## Scoring Guidelines

- Average support strength should reflect both the positivity and specificity of endorsements
- Consistency scores should reward alignment across multiple recommendations
- Depth of endorsement should emphasize specific examples and detailed observations
- Overall recommendation profile should consider both individual letter quality and aggregate strength

## Scholarship-Specific Considerations

### Recommender Preferences

**Priority Order:**
1. Flight school instructors (CFIs) - Highest priority
2. Aviation industry professionals (commercial pilots, ATC, maintenance, etc.)
3. Academic instructors with aviation focus
4. Employers in aviation-related fields
5. Other professional references

**Key Factors:**
- Recommendations from flight school instructors should be weighted more heavily
- Look for specific mentions of the applicant's flying abilities, safety awareness, and progress
- Value recommendations that demonstrate the instructor's direct observation of the applicant's aviation skills
- Consider the instructor's experience and credentials when available

### Additional Considerations

- Recommendations should ideally come from individuals who have directly observed the applicant's aviation-related work or training
- Generic recommendations or those that could apply to any applicant should be scored lower
- Recommendations that specifically address the scholarship's goals and the applicant's alignment with WAI values should be prioritized
